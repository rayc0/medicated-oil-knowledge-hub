## 1. What Is Fengyoujing?

Fengyoujing is one of the most iconic everyday topical medicines in China and among the global Chinese diaspora, often described alongside Qingliangou (清凉油, cooling balm) and Tiger Balm as the "three pillars of Chinese household medicated oils." Its name is descriptive: **"feng" (风)** refers to expelling wind-cold in traditional Chinese medicine, **"you" (油)** means oil (liquid formulation), and **"jing" (精)** means essence or concentrate — five active ingredients packed into a small, distinctively green bottle.

Unlike the solid or semi-solid wax balms popular in Southeast Asia, Fengyoujing is a **clear liquid**. This makes it fast-absorbing and easy to apply with a single drop; it evaporates within minutes, leaving a brief, intense cooling sensation followed by a mild warmth from its eugenol and camphor components.

Fengyoujing is not a single brand — dozens of manufacturers hold NMPA (National Medical Products Administration, 国家药品监督管理局) approval numbers under the 国药准字 Z (Chinese pharmaceutical registration Z-class) designation, sharing the same core formulation that is defined in China's national standard.

## 2. History and Origins

Fengyoujing's pharmacological lineage runs through the medicated oil traditions of Southeast Asia. The formulation's menthol-dominant, multi-terpene profile closely mirrors Eagle Brand (鹰标) products developed in Singapore in the early twentieth century, where warm, wet tropical conditions made cooling topical preparations highly practical.

The modern Chinese version of Fengyoujing was codified in the 1970s. In 1976, **Fujian Zhangzhou Narcissus Pharmaceutical (福建漳州水仙药业)** launched the first NMPA-registered Chinese Fengyoujing under the **Shuixian (水仙) brand**, with NMPA registration number 国药准字 Z35020385. The product was promoted nationally through China's planned-economy distribution system, becoming a household staple throughout the reform era of the 1980s and 1990s.

Today, Fengyoujing is produced by dozens of licensed manufacturers. The most widely distributed brands include:

| Brand | Manufacturer | Region |
|---|---|---|
| 水仙牌 Shuixian | Zhangzhou Narcissus Pharmaceutical, Fujian | Fujian, South China, Vietnam export |

Overseas, Vietnamese Dầu Gió ("Wind Oil") is a direct descendant of the same formulation, produced locally for Southeast Asian markets.

## 3. Formulation and Pharmacology

Fengyoujing achieves its clinical effects through five active terpene or phenylpropanoid compounds acting on complementary receptor pathways. The preferred standard formulation (per patent WO2004067017A1) is 320 g menthol, 260 g methyl salicylate, 30 g camphor, 30 g eucalyptus oil, 30 g eugenol, 100 g fragrance, 1 g chlorophyll, solvent to 1,000 g.

### 3.1 Menthol — ~32% (Dominant Active)

At 320 g per kilogram of formulation, menthol is Fengyoujing's defining ingredient and the highest-concentration component — significantly higher than Tiger Balm White (~11%), Wong To Yick Wood Lock Oil (~15%), or White Flower Oil (~15%).

**Mechanism:** Menthol selectively activates the **TRPM8** cold-sensing ion channel in cutaneous sensory neurons (McKemy et al., 2002, *Nature* 416:52–58; Peier et al., 2002, *Cell* 108:705–715). TRPM8 channel opening generates a "cold" signal transmitted to the brain — this is a neurochemical event, not an actual drop in skin temperature. Additionally, menthol inhibits TRPV1 to raise the pain threshold and acts as a non-competitive antagonist at 5-HT₃ receptors (Heimes et al., 2011, *Phytomedicine*), providing a mechanistic rationale for its traditional use in motion sickness and nausea.

**Clinical evidence:** A controlled RCT by Göbel et al. (1996, *Der Nervenarzt* 67:672–681) found that topical 10% peppermint oil (menthol-equivalent) applied to the temples produced tension-headache relief comparable to 1,000 mg oral paracetamol. Keh et al. (2011, *Rhinology* 49:29–36) confirmed TRPM8 activation in nasal mucosa, explaining the subjective sense of improved nasal patency.

### 3.2 Methyl Salicylate (Wintergreen Oil) — ~26%

Methyl salicylate is Fengyoujing's analgesic workhorse. At 26%, it is well above the concentration in many Asian medicated oils (Tiger Balm Red ~10%; Kwan Loong ~15%) — though still lower than Wong To Yick Wood Lock Oil's ~40%.

**Mechanism:** After topical application, methyl salicylate is absorbed through the skin and hydrolysed to **salicylic acid**, which provides mild COX-1/COX-2 inhibition, reducing prostaglandin synthesis. It also functions as a rubefacient (counterirritant) per FDA OTC Monograph 21 CFR Part 348 — inducing local vasodilation that, via Gate Control Theory, dampens the perception of deeper musculoskeletal pain. Transdermal absorption is meaningful: systemic exposure occurs, and is increased 3–5-fold under occlusion (Morra et al., 1996, *J Rheumatol* 23:1445–1449).

⚠️ **Toxicity note:** Methyl salicylate has a low oral lethal dose of approximately 101 mg/kg in humans. An adult ingesting 10–15 mL faces potentially fatal toxicity. **Oral use is strictly contraindicated.** Keep bottles sealed and out of children's reach.

### 3.3 Camphor — ~3%

**Mechanism:** Camphor activates **TRPV3** (Moqrich et al., 2005, *Science* 307:1468–1472) and **TRPA1** (Bandell et al., 2004, *Neuron* 41:849–857), producing a complex "cool then warm" sensation that complements the dominant menthol cooling. This gives Fengyoujing its characteristic layered feel.

⚠️ **Regulatory note:** FDA 21 CFR 310.545 limits camphor in OTC topical products to a maximum of 11%. Fengyoujing's ~3% is within this limit, but camphor at any concentration remains hazardous for infants and young children (Love et al., 2004, *J Emerg Med* 27:49–54, PMID 15219304).

### 3.4 Eucalyptus Oil (1,8-Cineole) — ~3%

The primary constituent 1,8-cineole weakly activates TRPM8, augmenting the cooling sensation, and provides mucolytic activity in the respiratory tract when inhaled. Objective studies show eucalyptus oil does not structurally relieve nasal congestion; the benefit is subjective (Keh 2011).

### 3.5 Eugenol (Clove Oil) — ~3%

Eugenol is a **TRPV1 agonist**, producing a mild warming and spicy sensation that gives Fengyoujing its distinctive aromatic depth — a "warm base note" beneath the menthol chill. It also contributes broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity (Kotan et al., 2007), providing incidental protection against secondary infection at insect-bite sites.

### 3.6 Excipients

- **Liquid paraffin (mineral oil):** inert carrier; keeps the formulation liquid and promotes even distribution of active terpenes
- **Chlorophyll:** colorant; provides the signature transparent green color; no pharmacological role
- **Fragrance:** olfactory modifier; no pharmacological role

## 4. Indications, Usage, and Dosing

### 4.1 Approved Indications and Evidence Tier

| Indication | Application Method | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Insect bites (itch relief) | Apply small amount to bite site | FDA Category I (menthol as topical antipruritic, 21 CFR Part 348) |
| Tension headache | Rub into temples and forehead | RCT support (Göbel 1996; Göbel 1994 *Cephalalgia*) |
| Cold-related headache / dizziness | Temples, nape of neck | Traditional use; symptom relief reported |
| Motion sickness | Before departure: apply to wrist, navel, or inhale briefly | Mechanistic rationale (menthol 5-HT₃ antagonism, Heimes 2011); no dedicated motion-sickness RCT |
| Nasal congestion | Apply near nostrils (not inside) | Subjective patency improvement (Keh 2011 Rhinology) |
| Minor muscle ache (small area, adult) | Massage into affected area 3–4×/day | FDA 21 CFR Part 348 counterirritant monograph |

### 4.2 How to Use

1. **External use only.** Never ingest, do not apply near eyes.
2. Apply a few drops — typically 3–5 drops — to the affected area and rub in gently.
3. **Limit application area.** Single-session coverage should not exceed one hand-palm area (~50 cm²). Larger areas increase systemic methyl salicylate absorption.
4. **Avoid occlusion and heat within 30 minutes** of application. Covering with plastic wrap or a heating pad increases percutaneous absorption 3–5-fold (Morra 1996).
5. Frequency: 3–4 times daily. Discontinue if skin redness, rash, or burning persists.
6. **Do not stack with other methyl salicylate products** (patches such as Salonpas, other liniments) on the same day — cumulative methyl salicylate load can enter a clinically significant range.

## 6. Safety Warnings

> **The following safety information is based on NMPA-approved labeling and peer-reviewed literature. It is mandatory content on this site for any ingredient-containing article.**

### 6.1 Infants and Young Children (< 3 Years): Absolute Contraindication ⛔

Infants have a significantly thinner stratum corneum and a higher body-surface-area-to-weight ratio, resulting in disproportionately high percutaneous absorption:

- **Menthol (32%):** Can cause laryngospasm and respiratory depression in infants. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP 1994, *Pediatrics*) advises against any camphor- or menthol-containing product for young children; Paul et al. (2010, *Pediatrics*) showed VapoRub (menthol/camphor/eucalyptus) produced no benefit and potential harm in children under 2.
- **Camphor (~3%):** Even at low concentrations can cause seizures in infants and toddlers (Love et al., 2004, *J Emerg Med* 27:49–54, PMID 15219304; FDA 21 CFR 310.545 banning camphor > 11% OTC).
- **Methyl salicylate (26%):** Oral ingestion of small volumes can be fatal in children (Chan 1996, *J Emerg Med*; Bell & Duggin 2002, *Med J Aust*). Even large-area topical application can produce systemic salicylate toxicity.

⛔ **Do not apply Fengyoujing to or near infants, and avoid heavy use in spaces shared with infants — inhaled menthol vapour poses a separate respiratory risk.**

### 6.2 Pregnancy: Contraindicated ⛔

- **Methyl salicylate (26%):** In the third trimester, salicylate metabolites inhibit COX, risking premature closure of the ductus arteriosus (Torloni et al., 2006, PMID 16355417; Bell & Duggin 2002, *Med J Aust* 176:210–211). ACOG (2015) advises avoiding all NSAIDs and salicylate-releasing compounds after 20 weeks gestation.
- **Camphor:** Crosses the placenta (AAP 1994). Associated with adverse fetal outcomes at high exposure.
- **NMPA labeling:** Explicitly lists pregnancy as a contraindication.

If you are pregnant and experience headache or nausea, consult your healthcare provider before using any medicated oil.

### 6.3 G6PD Deficiency (Favism): Contraindicated ⛔

Menthol is a known trigger for **haemolytic jaundice** in G6PD-deficient individuals (Olowe SA & Ransome-Kuti O, 1980, *Lancet* 2:967–969). At 32%, Fengyoujing represents a high menthol-exposure product. G6PD deficiency is most prevalent in populations from malaria-endemic regions: Southern China (Guangdong, Guangxi, Yunnan, Guizhou) 2–13% (male), Mediterranean, Middle East, Sub-Saharan Africa, and Southeast Asia.

If you or a family member has a confirmed G6PD diagnosis, avoid all menthol-containing medicated oils and consult a physician.

### 6.4 Broken or Irritated Skin: Contraindicated ⛔

Damaged skin eliminates the protective barrier function, increasing percutaneous methyl salicylate absorption by **3–5-fold** (Morra et al., 1996, *J Rheumatol*), raising the risk of systemic salicylate toxicity. Do not apply to open wounds, burns, eczematous, or otherwise broken skin.

### 6.5 Oral Ingestion: Strictly Forbidden ⛔

The minimum lethal oral dose of methyl salicylate in humans is approximately 101 mg/kg body weight — an adult ingesting 10–15 mL of Fengyoujing faces potentially fatal toxicity (Bell & Duggin 2002). Some older Chinese-language product inserts historically permitted diluted oral use; this guidance is obsolete and contradicted by current NMPA labeling and toxicological evidence. **Never ingest Fengyoujing.** Store with caps tightly secured, out of reach of children.

### 6.6 Anticoagulant Medications (Warfarin / NOACs): High Caution ⚠️

Large-area topical application of methyl salicylate products can potentiate warfarin anticoagulation by competitive displacement from protein-binding sites, producing clinically dangerous INR elevation (Joss & LeBlond, 2000, *Ann Pharmacother* 34:729–733, PMID 10860133; Chow et al., 1989, PMID 2778785). Patients on warfarin or other anticoagulants should inform their physician before using Fengyoujing, limit application area strictly, and watch for signs of unusual bleeding.

### 6.7 Cats: Toxic — Keep Away ⛔

Cats lack UDP-glucuronosyltransferase (UGT) and cannot metabolise camphor, menthol, or eucalyptus. Even trace exposure can cause hepatotoxicity (Court & Greenblatt, 1997, *J Vet Pharmacol Ther* 20:96–103). Do not apply Fengyoujing in spaces where cats are present, and wash hands thoroughly before handling cats.

## 7. Authenticity and Buying Guide

### 7.1 How to Verify a Genuine Product

All legitimate Chinese Fengyoujing products sold in China must carry a **国药准字 Z-prefixed approval number** on both the outer carton and bottle. You can verify any number at the [NMPA Drug Database](https://www.nmpa.gov.cn/datasearch/home-index.html) (search by approval number or manufacturer name).

**Five-point authenticity check:**

1. **Registration number present and verifiable** — if no 国药准字 number exists on packaging, do not buy
2. **Smell profile:** Genuine product has an intense menthol chill followed by a distinct clove (eugenol) warmth; counterfeits typically smell of synthetic peppermint fragrance only, without the eugenol depth note
3. **Color and clarity:** Clear, transparent green with no visible sediment or cloudiness
4. **Cap seal:** Original factory seal intact; no signs of refilling
5. **Price floor:** Below-cost pricing (less than CNY 3–5 for a 6 ml bottle) is a red flag

### 7.2 Where to Buy (Diaspora Markets)

In overseas Chinese communities, look for Fengyoujing at:
- **Asian supermarkets and herbalists:** Check for Chinese-language packaging with 国药准字 number
- **Established pharmacies in Chinatown districts:** Higher trust than informal markets
- **Online:** Major platforms (Amazon, Lazada) from established sellers with clear manufacturing information; avoid anonymous third-party listings

When purchasing outside China, most brands will lack local health authority registration (e.g., US FDA, UK MHRA, AU TGA). Personal-use import is generally tolerated, but commercially imported products should carry appropriate registration in the destination country. Always verify the 国药准字 number against the NMPA database regardless of purchase location.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: How large an area can I apply Fengyoujing to?**
Keep application to one palm-area (~50 cm²) per session. Covering with plastic wrap, a heat pad, or tight bandaging increases methyl salicylate absorption 3–5 fold (Morra 1996) and substantially raises the risk of systemic toxicity.

**Q2: What is the difference between Fengyoujing and Qingliangou (cooling balm)?**
The main differences: Fengyoujing is a liquid with ~32% menthol (strongest cooling) and ~26% methyl salicylate (analgesic); Qingliangou is a solid ointment with ~15% camphor as the dominant ingredient and no methyl salicylate, producing more warmth than cooling. Fengyoujing excels at instant cooling and pain relief; Qingliangou is better for wind-cold-type muscle ache.

**Q3: Can Fengyoujing help with motion sickness?**
There is a plausible mechanism: menthol's 5-HT₃ receptor antagonism (Heimes 2011) parallels the anti-nausea action of ondansetron. Traditionally, people apply a few drops to the wrist or navel before travel, or briefly inhale from the open bottle. No dedicated motion-sickness RCT exists for Fengyoujing specifically, but the mechanism is sound. Do not let children directly inhale from the bottle.

**Q4: I accidentally applied Fengyoujing to a large area and feel light-headed — what should I do?**
Remove clothing if saturated with the product. Wash the skin with soap and water. If symptoms include tinnitus, nausea, rapid breathing, or confusion, call emergency services — these may be signs of acute salicylate absorption.

**Q5: Can I use Fengyoujing together with a Salonpas or other analgesic patch?**
No. Both contain methyl salicylate. Simultaneous use compounds the total daily methyl salicylate absorbed systemically. Use one product at a time; observe the minimum 4-hour washout between applications.

**Q6: Why is Fengyoujing green?**
Chlorophyll is added as a colorant for brand identification — it has no pharmacological role. Slight variation in green shade between batches and brands is normal and does not indicate quality difference.

**Q7: Does Fengyoujing expire, and can I use it past the date?**
Do not use expired product. Methyl salicylate undergoes ester hydrolysis in warm, humid conditions, producing a sharp acidic smell (salicylic acid + methanol). Menthol oxidises, reducing cooling potency. If the bottle smells sour, vinegary, or off — or has been open for more than 24 months — discard it. Wrap in newspaper, seal in a plastic bag, and dispose in household solid waste (do not pour down drains or into recycling).

## 安全警示 / Safety Warning Summary

⚠️ **Absolute contraindications — no exceptions:**
- Children under 3 years of age (camphor + high-menthol risk)
- Pregnancy (methyl salicylate → salicylate → ductus arteriosus risk in T3; camphor crosses placenta)
- G6PD deficiency (32% menthol → haemolytic jaundice risk)
- Broken, damaged, or inflamed skin (3–5× absorption increase)
- Oral ingestion (methyl salicylate oral LD ~101 mg/kg)

⚠️ **Use with physician guidance:**
- Patients on warfarin or anticoagulants (INR potentiation risk)
- Asthma patients (strong menthol/eucalyptus vapour may trigger bronchospasm in sensitive individuals)
- Elderly patients on polypharmacy (impaired skin barrier increases absorption; drug interaction risk)

⚠️ **Environmental:**
- Do not use near cats (UGT deficiency → hepatotoxicity)
- Keep away from open flames (liquid paraffin base is flammable)
